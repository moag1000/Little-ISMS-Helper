# BCM — Mandatory Policy / Programme Document Set

> Specialist input for the Policy-Wizard feature, scope: BCMS document
> generation for tenants enabling BCM coverage.
> Standards covered: ISO 22301:2019, ISO 22313:2020 (guidance only), BSI
> 200-4:2023 (Business Continuity Management). Cross-mapping noted to ISO
> 27001:2022 A.5.29/A.5.30, EU-DORA Art. 11/12, BSI Grundschutz DER.4.
>
> Author role: BCM-Specialist
> Document scope: governance-level documents only. Operational records
> (BIA results, exercise reports, audit findings) are explicitly OUT of
> wizard scope and listed in §3.

---

## 1. Top-Level BCM Policy

### Standard reference
- **ISO 22301:2019 Cl. 5.2** "Policy" — explicit `shall` requirement
  ("Top management shall establish a business continuity policy that …").
- **ISO 22301:2019 Cl. 5.1** "Leadership and commitment" — defines who
  must approve.
- **BSI 200-4 (2023) Kap. 4.1** "Notfallleitlinie" — German equivalent,
  identical material requirement, slightly stricter wording on
  Top-Management-Bekenntnis.
- **BSI 200-4 Kap. 4.4** "Verantwortlichkeiten" — names the
  Notfallbeauftragte/r role (BCM Officer).

### Document name
- **English (canonical):** Business Continuity Policy
- **German (suggested):** Notfallleitlinie / Leitlinie zum
  Business Continuity Management
- BSI 200-4 forces the term **"Notfallleitlinie"** in regulated
  environments — for German-language tenants this term wins over
  "BCM-Leitlinie".

### Document type
- **Policy** (governance directive). Sits at the apex of the BCMS
  document pyramid alongside the BCMS Scope Statement.

### Required content blocks (per ISO 22301 Cl. 5.2 a–f)
1. **Purpose** — why the organisation does BCM.
2. **Scope reference** — points to BCMS Scope Statement (separate
   document, see §2.1).
3. **BCMS objectives** — measurable, derived from organisational
   context (Cl. 4.1) and interested-party requirements (Cl. 4.2).
4. **Top-management commitment** — explicit clause ("Die
   Geschäftsführung verpflichtet sich…"), with named accountable
   executive.
5. **Roles & responsibilities** — at minimum: top management, BCM
   Officer / Notfallbeauftragte/r, Crisis Team Chair, BC Plan owners.
6. **Compliance commitment** — applicable laws, regulations,
   contractual obligations (DORA / KRITIS / sector-specific).
7. **Continual improvement commitment** — links to Cl. 10.1 procedure.
8. **Review & approval signatures** — top management explicit sign-off.
9. **Communication clause** — how the policy is disseminated (BSI 200-4
   Kap. 4.1 specifically demands `aktive Kommunikation`).

### Approval chain
- **Required signer:** Top management (ISO 22301 Cl. 5.1) — typically
  CEO / Geschäftsführung. NOT the CISO. NOT the BCM Officer.
- The BCM Officer drafts, top management approves.
- For multi-tenant holdings: parent-level Notfallleitlinie may be
  inherited; subsidiary top management must still formally adopt
  (record of adoption, not new signature on parent document).

### Review cadence
- **ISO 22301 Cl. 9.3:** Management review at planned intervals — no
  fixed period, common practice = annual.
- **BSI 200-4 Kap. 4.1:** explicitly `mindestens jährlich`.
- **Trigger-based reviews** (mandatory regardless of cadence):
  - Major organisational change (M&A, new site, divestiture)
  - Significant incident invocation
  - Regulatory landscape change (new DORA technical standard, NIS2
    transposition, etc.)
  - Failed exercise / lessons-learned with strategic impact

### Tenant-settings inputs needed
- `organisation_legal_name` (already in tenant profile — reuse)
- `bcm_scope_statement_ref` (link to BCMS Scope document — see §2.1)
- `bcm_officer_name` + `bcm_officer_role_title` + `bcm_officer_email`
- `bcm_officer_deputy_name` (BSI 200-4 explicitly recommends a deputy)
- `top_management_approver_name` + `top_management_approver_role`
- `bcm_objectives[]` — list of measurable objectives, free-text +
  optional KPI link
- `applicable_regulations[]` — checkboxes (DORA / NIS2 / KRITIS / TKG /
  sectoral) — drives the compliance-commitment paragraph
- `policy_review_cadence_months` (default 12, BSI tenants forced to ≤12)

### 2.2 BIA Methodology

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Standard ref | ISO 22301 Cl. 8.2.2 (`shall implement and maintain a process`) |
| Document name (EN) | Business Impact Analysis Methodology |
| Document name (DE) | BIA-Methodik / Methodik der Business-Impact-Analyse |
| Document type | **Methodology** (defines HOW, not the analysis itself) |
| Linked entities | BusinessProcess (target of BIA); Asset (dependencies) |
| Review cadence | every 2 years OR on methodology change (BSI 200-4 Kap. 5.2.1) |

**Required content sections (per Cl. 8.2.2 a–f):**
- Impact categories (financial, regulatory, reputational, life-safety,
  operational) — must be defined BEFORE BIA execution
- Impact-rating scale (e.g. 1–5 with monetary anchors)
- Time-windows for impact assessment (1h / 4h / 24h / 72h / 1week /
  1month — typical, organisation-specific)
- Definition: **MTPD** (Maximum Tolerable Period of Disruption)
- Definition: **RTO** (Recovery Time Objective) — must be < MTPD
- Definition: **RPO** (Recovery Point Objective)
- Definition: **MBCO** (Minimum Business Continuity Objective)
- Dependency-discovery method (upstream / downstream / lateral)
- Data-collection technique (workshop / interview / questionnaire)
- Roles: BIA-Lead, Process-Owner, Reviewer
- Review and sign-off rules
- Re-execution trigger (org change, new product, post-exercise)

**Tenant-settings inputs:**
- `bia_impact_categories[]` (default 5 categories preselected, editable)
- `bia_impact_scale_levels` (default 5, range 3–7)
- `bia_time_windows[]` (default `[1h,4h,24h,72h,1w,1mo]`)
- `bia_collection_method` (workshop / interview / questionnaire)
- `bia_review_cycle_months` (default 24)

> (a) `reuse_isms_methodology = true` — generates a 1-page document

> (b) `reuse_isms_methodology = false` — generates full BCM-RA
>     methodology document.

### 2.4 BC Strategy / Solutions Document

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Standard ref | ISO 22301 Cl. 8.3 |
| Document name (EN) | Business Continuity Strategy and Solutions |
| Document name (DE) | BCM-Strategie und Lösungsalternativen |
| Document type | **Programme** (high-level, covers entire BCMS) |
| Linked entities | BusinessProcess (each in-scope), Asset, Supplier |
| Review cadence | annual; trigger on BIA-update or major architecture change |

**Required content sections (per Cl. 8.3.2):**
- Strategic options per criticality tier:
  - **Critical processes** (RTO ≤ 4h): hot-standby / dual-site / cloud-failover
  - **Important processes** (RTO ≤ 24h): warm-standby / contracted recovery
  - **Standard processes** (RTO ≤ 72h): cold-standby / manual workaround
  - **Deferrable processes** (RTO > 72h): document and accept
- Resource categories addressed (Cl. 8.3.3 a–g):
  - People (succession, cross-training, evacuation)
  - Information (backup, retention, classification)
  - Buildings, work environment & utilities
  - Facilities, equipment & consumables
  - ICT systems
  - Transportation & logistics
  - Finance
  - Partners & suppliers (sub-strategy: supplier diversification)
- Cost / benefit summary per option
- Selected strategy per process — references BC Plan §2.5 by name
- **Risk-treatment-link** — strategies that mitigate identified BCM risks

**Tenant-settings inputs:**
- `criticality_tiers[]` — list of tiers + RTO/MTPD per tier
- `default_strategy_per_tier` (mapping)
- `cloud_failover_available` (yes/no — drives template options)
- `secondary_site` (Location-entity ref, optional)
- `supplier_diversification_required` (yes/no — for tier-1 processes)

### 2.8 Recovery Plans

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Standard ref | ISO 22301 Cl. 8.4.5; BSI 200-4 Kap. 6.2 (Wiederanlauf-/Wiederherstellungspläne) |
| Document name (EN) | Recovery Plan Standard |
| Document name (DE) | Wiederanlauf- und Wiederherstellungsplan-Standard |
| Document type | **Procedure** (defines plan-authoring rules; individual recovery plans are records linked per-asset) |
| Linked entities | Asset (each critical IT-Asset gets a recovery plan), BCPlan |

**Required content sections:**

  have a linked recovery plan
- Linkage to backup procedures (existing BackupService scope)
- Test-evidence requirement

**BSI 200-4 distinction (Kap. 6) — IMPORTANT:**
The wizard MUST surface this in DE-tenants:

| Term (DE) | Term (EN) | Trigger | Goal |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Geschäftsfortführungsplan** | Business Continuation Plan | Notfall (process disruption) | Maintain/resume business operation via workarounds |
| **Wiederanlaufplan** | Recovery Plan (resumption) | Bring resources back to operating state, possibly degraded | Restart services |
| **Wiederherstellungsplan** | Restoration Plan | After recovery; long-term | Restore full pre-incident state |

The English ISO 22301 single-term "BC Plan" conflates the first two;
BSI 200-4 explicitly demands all three. For BSI-only tenants, wizard
must generate three separate document templates.

- `recovery_plan_split_bsi` (boolean — default = true if BSI in
  `standards_adopted`)

## 5. Hierarchy Considerations

For tenants using the Holding/Konzern feature:

- **Konzern-Notfallleitlinie** (parent-level Notfallleitlinie):
  - Defines group-wide BCM principles, minimum RTO-tiers,
    Crisis-Team-coordination rules
  - Approved by Konzern-Geschäftsführung
  - Subsidiaries inherit by reference; subsidiary top-management adopts
    via short adoption-record (NOT a new full Notfallleitlinie)
  - Wizard at subsidiary-level: detect Konzern-Leitlinie present →
    offer "inherit + delta-document" option

- **Konzern-BCM-Officer** (group-level):
  - Coordinates across subsidiaries, reports to group top-management
  - Subsidiaries have local BCM-Officer or deputy — `Notfallbeauftragte/r`
  - Role mapping in app: existing `ROLE_GROUP_CISO` analogue —
    backlog: introduce `ROLE_GROUP_BCM_OFFICER` (out of wizard scope but
    flag for product)

- **Cross-border BCM:**
  - Each jurisdiction's regulator may demand local-language BC plans
    (especially DE BSI-Kritis)
  - DORA Art. 11 imposes EU-level requirement; local regulators may
    demand additional notification thresholds
  - Wizard input: `subsidiary_jurisdictions[]` drives a per-jurisdiction
    annex generation (NOT separate documents — annex chapter is
    sufficient for governance level)

- **Override rules:**
  - Subsidiary may NOT relax parent's RTO targets (downgrade blocked
    in UI)
  - Subsidiary MAY tighten RTO targets (specialised business)
  - Subsidiary MUST inherit parent's notification thresholds AT
    MINIMUM
  - Subsidiary MUST adopt parent's Crisis-Team-coordination rules
    (no parallel Crisis-Team without parent-Crisis-Team-coordination)
  - Field validators: `rto_hours <= parent_rto_hours` enforced

- **Document numbering:**
  - Konzern-documents: `BCM-K-001` (Konzern Notfallleitlinie),
    `BCM-K-002` (Konzern Krisenstabsordnung) etc.
  - Subsidiary documents: `BCM-T<tenant_id>-001` etc.
  - Inheritance audit-trail: each subsidiary document references the
    parent document version it derives from

## 7. Wizard-Specific Risks

### 7.1 Organisation-specificity gap
BCM is heavily organisation-specific. Generic templates feel hollow if
tenant hasn't done process discovery. Mitigation:
- Wizard MUST require at least one BusinessProcess record in scope
  before generation — enforced via Step-1 validator.
- BIA-Methodology document MUST reference actual BusinessProcess names
  in its example section (auto-inserted).

### 7.2 Auditor-trap: BCMS without exercise evidence
External auditors flag BCMS without exercise-records as
**nonconformity (major)**. ISO 22301 Cl. 8.6 is a `shall`. Mitigation:
- Wizard MUST generate Exercise Programme document (§2.9), not
  optional.
- Wizard SHOULD auto-create scheduled BCExercise entity records for
  the next 12 months (`auto_create_bcexercise_records_12m = true` by
  default).
- Wizard final-step lists "exercise execution next 90 days" as
  follow-up obligation.

### 7.3 BIA seeding from BusinessProcess
BIA needs actual data; wizard CAN auto-pull from BusinessProcess
entities to seed a BIA worksheet. Recommendation: **YES**.
- BusinessProcess records have `criticality` field already → seed
  initial BIA-tier mapping
- BusinessProcess `dependencies[]` → seed dependency-discovery section
- BusinessProcess `owner` → seed BIA-data-collection contact list
- BIA-execution itself remains out of THIS wizard scope, but seeded
  worksheet eliminates blank-page problem

### 7.4 ROC (Recovery Objective Coverage)
For each in-scope BusinessProcess, RTO target MUST be set BEFORE BC
plan template can be meaningfully generated. Mitigation:
- Wizard Step-4 (Continuity Objectives) enforces `rto_hours` for every
  in-scope BusinessProcess
- Validation rule: `count(processes_without_rto) == 0` before
  generation
- If user has not yet completed BIA, wizard offers a "use criticality
  defaults" shortcut: `criticality=critical → 4h`, `high → 24h`,
  `medium → 72h`, `low → 1 week` — flagged as PROVISIONAL with
  red-banner reminder to refine after BIA execution.

### 7.5 BSI vs. ISO terminology drift
BSI 200-4 distinguishes Geschäftsfortführungsplan / Wiederanlaufplan /
Wiederherstellungsplan; ISO 22301 collapses to "BC plans". For dual-
standard tenants the wizard must avoid generating BOTH ISO-style
single-plan AND BSI-style three-plans (duplicate work). Resolution:
- If `bsi_200_4 in standards_adopted`: generate the three-plan-split
  (BSI wins, more granular)
- If only `iso_22301`: generate the single-plan template
- Documents always carry ISO-22301 clause-mapping in header so dual-
  audit works

### 7.6 Crisis-Team singleton risk
If tenant has not yet defined CrisisTeam entity, Crisis-Mgmt-Plan
generation will produce an empty placeholder. Mitigation:
- Wizard Step-3 prompts for Crisis-Team composition (5–7 roles
  minimum, BSI requires deputies)
- On wizard completion, wizard creates/updates a CrisisTeam entity
  with the provided composition
- Crisis-Mgmt-Plan document references this entity ID — keeps single
  source of truth

### 7.7 Document approval bottleneck
BCM Policy (§1) demands top-management signature. Wizards generate
draft only — but tenants frequently miss the approval step.
Mitigation:
- Generated BCM Policy auto-creates an approval task (Workflow
  instance, type = `policy_approval`, assignee = top-management
  approver from Step-2)
- Document status remains `draft` until top-management confirms
- Alva-hint: 30 days post-generation, if Policy still draft → reminder

### 7.8 Translation completeness
BSI source material is German-original; some terms have no clean
English equivalent (`Notfallbeauftragte/r`, `Krisenstabsordnung`).
Wizard must use German source-terms in glossary section even in EN
documents. Risk: AI-translation degrades regulator-recognition.
Mitigation: maintain manual translation glossary in
`translations/bcm.de.yaml` + `translations/bcm.en.yaml` with explicit
"keep-as-is" markers for regulator-specific German terms.

## 8. Ordering / Dependencies

Wizard MUST enforce the following document-generation ORDER. Skipping
upstream blocks downstream (or generates placeholder-only).

```
1. BCM Policy (§1) + BCMS Scope Statement (§2.1)
   ├── (output: top-of-pyramid governance)
   │
2. BIA Methodology (§2.2)
   ├── (defines criticality measurement)
   │
3. [GATE] BIA Execution    ← OUT OF WIZARD SCOPE
   ├── If skipped: downstream uses provisional criticality from
   │   BusinessProcess.criticality, with red-banner warning
   │
4. BCM Risk Assessment Methodology (§2.3)
   ├── References BIA-criticality
   │
5. BC Strategy / Solutions (§2.4)
   ├── Requires BIA-tiers AND BCM-RA results (or provisional)
   │
6. BC Plan Template Standard (§2.5)
   └── Recovery Plan Standard (§2.8)
   ├── Requires BC Strategy
   │
7. Incident Response & Communication Procedure (§2.6)
   └── Crisis Management Plan (§2.7)
   ├── Independent of BIA but logical placement after BC plans
   │   (because notification thresholds tie to RTOs)
   │
8. Exercise & Testing Programme (§2.9)
   ├── Requires BC Plans + Crisis Mgmt Plan exist (to be exercised)
   │
9. Internal Audit Programme (§2.10) + Management Review (§2.11) +
   Nonconformity / CAR Procedure (§2.12)
   ├── Independent — can run in parallel
   │
10. (BSI-only) Notfallhandbuch (§2.13)
    └── References ALL of the above — generate LAST as bundling doc
```

**Validation rules in wizard:**
- `bcm_scope_processes` non-empty before §2.4 generation
- BIA-tiers defined (rto_hours per tier) before §2.4, §2.5, §2.8
- CrisisTeam composition defined before §2.7
- Exercise frequencies > 0 before §2.9

**Generation strategy:**
Wizard generates ALL documents in a single transaction (atomic). If
generation fails mid-way, ALL documents are rolled back to avoid
half-state. EntityManager wrap with try-catch and check `$em->isOpen()`
per project pitfall #1.

## 9. Recommendations

### 9.1 Wizard scope split
- **THIS wizard ("BCM-Policy-Wizard"):** generates governance / programme
  / methodology / template documents (§1, §2.1–§2.4, §2.6, §2.7, §2.9–§2.13).
- **EXISTING BCPlan-Wizard / BCPlan-form:** generates per-process BC
  Plan content (operational level).
- **OUT-OF-SCOPE:** BIA execution, Risk-Assessment execution,
  Exercise execution — these need separate workflows / forms.
- Clear naming: this wizard is "BCMS Foundation Wizard" or
  "BCM-Policy-Wizard" — NOT "BCM-Wizard" (too broad, confuses scope).

### 9.2 Document storage & translation keys
- Each generated document opens in Document module with `status =
  draft` and template translation key `bcm.template.<doc_slug>`
- Translation domain: `bcm` (already exists) — extend with
  `bcm.template.*` namespace
- Document-classification default = `Internal-Confidential` (BSI 200-4
  requirement); BCM Policy may be `Public-Internal` (some orgs
  publish summary version)

### 9.3 Auto-link to existing entities
- **CrisisTeam:** if entity already exists for tenant → link;
  otherwise wizard creates one with Step-3 composition
- **BCPlan:** wizard creates empty BCPlan record per in-scope
  BusinessProcess with `status=draft, owner=<process_owner>`
- **BCExercise:** if `auto_create_bcexercise_records_12m = true`, wizard
  creates planned BCExercise records for next 12 months per chosen
  frequency
- **BusinessProcess:** READ-only — wizard pulls scope/criticality, never
  modifies process records (separation of concerns)
- **Asset:** READ-only — wizard pulls high-criticality Assets to seed
  Recovery-Plan-list section, never modifies

### 9.4 Workflow integration
- Generated BCM Policy auto-creates `policy_approval` workflow
  (existing pattern from ISMS-Wizard)
- Generated Crisis-Mgmt-Plan auto-creates a "first-tabletop-exercise"
  task due within 90 days (drives §7.2 mitigation)
- Generated Exercise Programme auto-creates BCExercise records as
  workflow-trigger source

### 9.5 Compliance Wizard integration
The existing compliance-wizard infrastructure (per recent commits:
ISO 22301 wizard, ISO 27701, NIS2 etc.) already has check-types like
`bia_coverage`, `dora_artikel11_coverage`. The Policy-Wizard should
register check-types:
- `bcm_policy_present` (true if §1 generated and approved)
- `bcm_scope_documented` (true if §2.1 generated)
- `bcm_bia_methodology_present` (true if §2.2 generated)
- `bcm_strategy_documented` (true if §2.4 generated)
- `bcm_exercise_programme_present` (true if §2.9 generated)
- `bcm_management_review_documented` (true if §2.11 generated)
- This enables the existing ISO-22301-Compliance-Wizard to verify
  policy-coverage automatically.

### 9.6 Alva-Hint integration
Per project memory `project_alva_hint_foundation`, register hints:
- `bcm.policy_draft_overdue` (Tier-2): >30 days, Policy still
  `status=draft`
- `bcm.no_exercise_planned` (Tier-1): no BCExercise scheduled in next
  12 months
- `bcm.bia_outdated` (Tier-2): >24 months since last BIA-Methodology
  review
- `bcm.crisis_team_incomplete` (Tier-1): CrisisTeam <5 members or
  missing deputy
- `bcm.notfallhandbuch_missing_bsi` (Tier-1): BSI in standards but no
  Notfallhandbuch
