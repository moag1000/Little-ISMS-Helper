# EU DORA - Complete Reference Document

**Regulation (EU) 2022/2554** - Digital Operational Resilience Act

## Official Information

- **Full Title**: Regulation (EU) 2022/2554 of the European Parliament and of the Council of 14 December 2022 on digital operational resilience for the financial sector
- **Adopted**: December 14, 2022
- **Published**: Official Journal L 333, December 27, 2022
- **Entry into Force**: January 16, 2023
- **Application Date**: ✅ **January 17, 2025** (fully applicable)
- **Official Text**: https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2022/2554/oj
- **Current Status (November 2025)**: Fully enforced, active supervision ongoing

## Scope of Application

### In-Scope Entities (Article 2)

**Financial Entities:**
1. Credit institutions (Article 3(1)(1) CRR)
2. Payment institutions (Directive (EU) 2015/2366)
3. Account information service providers
4. Electronic money institutions (Directive 2009/110/EC)
5. Investment firms (Directive 2014/65/EU)
6. Crypto-asset service providers (Regulation (EU) 2023/1114)
7. Central securities depositories (Regulation (EU) No 909/2014)
8. Central counterparties (Regulation (EU) No 648/2012)
9. Trading venues (Directive 2014/65/EU)
10. Trade repositories (Regulation (EU) No 648/2012)
11. Managers of alternative investment funds (Directive 2011/61/EU)
12. Management companies (Directive 2009/65/EC)
13. Data reporting service providers (Directive 2014/65/EU)
14. Insurance and reinsurance undertakings (Directive 2009/138/EC)
15. Insurance intermediaries (Directive (EU) 2016/97)
16. Institutions for occupational retirement provision (Directive (EU) 2016/2341)
17. Credit rating agencies (Regulation (EC) No 1060/2009)
18. Administrators of critical benchmarks (Regulation (EU) 2016/1011)
19. Crowdfunding service providers (Regulation (EU) 2020/1503)
20. Securitisation repositories (Regulation (EU) 2017/2402)

**ICT Third-Party Service Providers:**
- Providers of ICT services to financial entities
- Subject to oversight if designated as "critical" (Article 31)

### Microenterprises Exception (Article 4)
- Microenterprises (as defined in Commission Recommendation 2003/361/EC) are subject to simplified regime
- <10 employees AND annual turnover or balance sheet ≤€2M

## Structure of DORA

### Chapter I: General Provisions (Articles 1-4)
- Article 1: Subject matter
- Article 2: Scope
- Article 3: Definitions (53 definitions)
- Article 4: Proportionality principle

### Chapter II: ICT Risk Management (Articles 5-16)

**Section I: General Rules (Articles 5-10)**

**Article 5: Governance and Organisation**
- Management body responsibility
- ICT risk management framework approval
- Business continuity policy
- ICT budget allocation

**Article 6: ICT Systems, Protocols and Tools**
- ICT systems with appropriate capacity
- Up-to-date ICT systems
- Minimise ICT risk
- Ensure continuity of critical functions
- **Mapping**: ISO 27001 A.8.1, A.8.9, A.8.16, A.8.19

**Article 7: Identification**
- Identify all ICT-supported business functions
- ICT assets and configurations
- Dependencies between functions and assets
- **Mapping**: ISO 27001 A.5.9, A.5.10

**Article 8: Protection and Prevention**
- Policies for data and ICT infrastructure
- Mechanisms to protect systems
- Regular testing of security measures
- Evaluation of effectiveness
- **Mapping**: ISO 27001 A.8.5, A.8.24, A.8.23

**Article 9: Detection**
- Mechanisms to detect anomalous activities
- Predefined thresholds for incidents
- Monitoring of critical systems
- **Mapping**: ISO 27001 A.8.16

**Article 10: Response and Recovery**
- Dedicated plans for responses
- Restoration procedures
- Communication plans
- **Mapping**: ISO 27001 A.5.24-A.5.28, A.5.29-A.5.30

**Section II: Simplified ICT Risk Management Framework (Article 16)**
- For microenterprises and small financial entities
- Simplified requirements while maintaining core objectives

### Chapter III: ICT-Related Incident Management, Classification and Reporting (Articles 17-23)

**Article 17: ICT-Related Incident Management Process**
- Detection, management, and notification procedures
- Recording of incidents in registers
- Establishment of response procedures

**Article 18: Classification of ICT-Related Incidents**
- Major incidents vs. significant cyber threats
- Criteria: impact, duration, geographical spread, data losses, criticality

**Article 19: Voluntary Reporting**
- Option to report significant cyber threats
- Anonymised reporting possible

**Article 20: Harmonised Reporting of Major ICT-Related Incidents**
- **Initial notification**: Significant impact becomes apparent (**≤4 hours**)
- **Intermediate report**: Regular updates
- **Final report**: Within 1 month after initial notification

**Article 21: Centralised Reporting**
- Incidents reported to competent authorities
- Information shared with ESAs, ECB, ESRB

**Article 22: Voluntary Notification of Significant Cyber Threats**
- Financial entities may notify about threats not yet materialised

**Article 23: Details of Reports**
- Content specified in ITS (2024/1502)
- Use of standardised templates

### Chapter IV: Digital Operational Resilience Testing (Articles 24-27)

**Article 24: General Requirements for Testing**
- Regular testing of ICT systems
- Different testing methodologies
- Vulnerability assessments
- Scenario-based testing

**Article 25: Requirements for Advanced Testing**
- Specific financial entities must conduct advanced testing
- Tests cover critical functions
- Assess preparedness for cyberattacks

**Article 26: Threat-Led Penetration Testing (TLPT)**
- **Frequency**: Every 3 years minimum
- **Scope**: Live production systems
- **Testers**: Qualified and certified
- **Methodology**: Intelligence-led, mimicking real attack tactics
- **Cooperation**: Involvement of internal ICT staff
- **Detailed in**: Commission Delegated Regulation (EU) 2024/1774

**Article 27: Requirements for Testers**
- Expertise, integrity, independence
- Certification schemes

### Chapter V: Managing of ICT Third-Party Risk (Articles 28-44)

**Section I: Key Principles and General Provisions (Articles 28-30)**

**Article 28: General Principles**
- ICT third-party risk as part of ICT risk
- Contractual arrangements proportionate to risk
- Risk assessment before entering agreements

**Article 29: Preliminary Assessment**
- Due diligence before entering ICT contracts
- Assessment of provider capabilities
- Concentration risk analysis

**Article 30: Key Contractual Provisions**
Must include:
- Full description of services
- Locations of data processing
- Service level requirements
- Audit rights for authorities
- Termination and exit strategies
- **Detailed in**: Commission Delegated Regulation (EU) 2024/1932 (Subcontracting)

**Section II: Oversight Framework (Articles 31-44)**

**Article 31: Designation of Critical ICT Third-Party Providers**
- ESAs jointly designate CTPPs
- Criteria: systemic importance, substitutability, number of financial entities served
- **Current Status (November 2025)**: 19 CTPPs designated on November 18, 2025

**Article 32: Structure of Oversight Framework**
- Lead Overseer appointed by ESAs
- Oversight activities coordinated

**Article 33-44: Oversight Mechanisms**
- General requests for information (Art. 35)
- On-site inspections (Art. 37)
- Off-site supervision
- Recommendations (Art. 42)
- Penalties up to €5M or 1% of total worldwide turnover (Art. 35)

### Chapter VI: Information Sharing Arrangements (Articles 45-49)

**Article 45: Information Sharing Arrangements**
- Financial entities may exchange cyber threat intelligence
- Effective mechanisms for sharing

**Article 46: Conditions**
- Trust frameworks
- Legitimate interest

**Article 47: Participants**
- Financial entities, public authorities, service providers

### Chapter VII: Competent Authorities (Articles 50-54)

**Article 50: Competent Authorities**
- Designation by Member States
- Powers and responsibilities

**Article 51: Lead Overseer**
- Appointed by ESAs for each CTPP
- Coordination of oversight activities

## Key Definitions (Article 3)

**ICT Risk**: Any reasonably identifiable circumstance in relation to the use of network and information systems which, if materialised, may compromise the security of the network and information systems, of any technology dependent tool or process, of operations and processes, or of the provision of services.

**ICT-Related Incident**: An event that compromises the availability, authenticity, integrity or confidentiality of stored, transmitted or processed data or of the services offered by the network and information systems.

**Major ICT-Related Incident**: An ICT-related incident that has a high adverse impact on the network and information systems that support critical or important functions.

**Significant Cyber Threat**: A cyber threat the technical characteristics of which indicate that it could have the potential to result in a major ICT-related incident.

**Critical or Important Function**: A function, the disruption of which would materially impair the financial performance, the soundness or continuity of the services and activities, or the discontinued, defective or failed performance of that function would materially impair the continuing compliance with the conditions and obligations of an authorisation.

**ICT Third-Party Service Provider**: An undertaking providing ICT services.

**Critical ICT Third-Party Service Provider**: An ICT third-party service provider designated as critical in accordance with Article 31.

## Penalties (Article 50)

### Financial Entities
- **Serious breaches**: Up to 2% of total annual worldwide turnover
- **Other breaches**: Up to 1% of total annual worldwide turnover
- **Minimum**: €500,000 (for breaches of reporting obligations)

### Critical ICT Third-Party Providers
- **Maximum**: €5 million or 1% of average daily worldwide turnover
- **Factors**: Severity, duration, cooperation, intentional/negligent

## Transitional Provisions (Article 57)

- **No general transitional period**: Full application from January 17, 2025
- **Exception**: TLPT (Article 26) - First test to be performed within 3 years of application date

## Implementation Checklist

### For Financial Entities

**Phase 1: Governance (Articles 5-6)**
- [ ] Management body approves ICT risk management framework
- [ ] ICT strategy aligned with business strategy
- [ ] ICT risk tolerance defined
- [ ] Business continuity policy established
- [ ] Annual ICT budget allocated

**Phase 2: ICT Risk Management (Articles 7-10)**
- [ ] Inventory of ICT assets (Art. 7)
- [ ] Identify critical/important functions
- [ ] Protection mechanisms implemented (Art. 8)
- [ ] Detection capabilities established (Art. 9)
- [ ] Response & recovery plans documented (Art. 10)

**Phase 3: Incident Management (Articles 17-23)**
- [ ] Incident classification criteria defined
- [ ] 4-hour initial notification capability
- [ ] Incident register established
- [ ] Reporting templates implemented (ITS 2024/1502)

**Phase 4: Testing (Articles 24-27)**
- [ ] Annual testing program defined
- [ ] TLPT planned (if applicable) - every 3 years
- [ ] Vulnerability assessments scheduled
- [ ] Scenario-based testing included

**Phase 5: Third-Party Risk (Articles 28-30)**
- [ ] Register of information created (ITS 2024/1689)
- [ ] Due diligence process for new providers
- [ ] Existing contracts reviewed for DORA clauses
- [ ] Exit strategies documented
- [ ] Concentration risk assessed

**Phase 6: Documentation & Evidence**
- [ ] ICT risk management framework documented
- [ ] Policies and procedures approved
- [ ] Audit trail for incidents
- [ ] Testing reports archived
- [ ] Third-party contracts DORA-compliant

**Phase 7: Ongoing Compliance**
- [ ] Quarterly management reviews
- [ ] Annual framework updates
- [ ] Continuous monitoring
- [ ] Regular staff training
